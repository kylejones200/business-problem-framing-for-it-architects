### Business problem framing for IT Architects
#### Problem framing is like eating vegetables. You know it's good for you but no one likes to do it.
It is something that most people just skip over and jump immediately and
to trying to solve the problem. However, that will always lead to
suboptimal results because you're not necessarily solving the right
problem. It is possible to solve analysis problem and someone actually
resolved the business issue. Did you solve the wrong problem?


<figcaption>Photo by Kelly Sikkema on Unsplash</figcaption>


Getting to the business problem is hard because people don't often know
what it is. You have to ask questions and dig deep to find out what is
the underlying issue that we're trying to solve. One method is called
the five whys. You asked why five times. Q: What do you want to do? A:
Reduce customer churn. Q: Why? A: Because customer
churn reduces revenue. Q: Why? A: Because it means we have fewer
customers and we have to get new customers...

Now we have to start to say all right problem is actually getting
retaining customers not predicting customer churn.

Our job is IT Architects is to require that this step happens in the
process. Even if people don't want to do it if they make them beat their
vegetables.
